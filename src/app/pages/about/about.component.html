About Me

I'm an ambitious software engineering student hailing from Amman, Jordan, currently studying for my Bachelor's degree at the Budapest University of Technology and Economics (BME). My interests in the field of computer science are mostly related to the applications of data science and machine learning.

My major is called Computer Science Engineering, neither purely Computer Science nor Computer Engineering. Think of Computer Engineering as a blend of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science. Now, mix in even more Computer Science, and you get Computer Science Engineering. In BME, there's a "specialization system", so I'm currently specialized in Software Engineering.

Throughout my studies, I worked on various projects that helped me gain experience in different fields of computer science. I'm heading the Tech Team of the Google Developer Student Club at BME, where we organize both technical and non-technical events for students, and I'm also a Student Mentor, member of BME Sollertia, and a Teaching Assistant for the Databases subject at the university.

In my free time, I play football, lift weights, game, and enjoy reading philosophy.

 BME - Budapest University of Technology and Economics

Fun Fact: I studied Civil Engineering for a year before switching to Computer Science Engineering, yet I studied both in Budapest University of Technology and Economics (BME), which was founded in 1782. The university was the first institute in Europe to train engineers at university level.

BME is ranked around 730 in the world according to QS, which evaluates only the top-performing universities globally. While there are around 50,000 universities worldwide, QS ranks just the top 3% of them. This means BME is already competing among the best institutions internationally, reinforcing its strong global reputation.
